Process

Linen is derived from the flax plant. The process starts with cultivating flax, which is typically grown in cooler climates. Flax plants are ready for harvest when they begin to flower. The flax plants are harvested when they reach maturity, usually about 100 days after planting. Harvesting involves pulling up the entire plant, roots and all, to obtain the longest fibers.
